Understanding AIFs

Three Regulatory Categories. Distinct Mandates.

Alternative Investment Funds are pooled vehicles registered under SEBI (AIF) Regulations, 2012. Each category serves a distinct investment purpose and investor profile.

Category I AIF
Venture & Social Impact

Funds that invest in start-ups, early-stage ventures, social ventures, SMEs, infrastructure, and other sectors which the government or regulators consider socially or economically desirable.

Venture Capital Angel Funds SME Funds Infrastructure
Category II AIF
Private Equity & Debt

Funds that do not fall in Category I or III and do not undertake leverage or borrowing other than to meet day-to-day operational requirements. Includes PE, real estate, and debt funds.

Private Equity Pre-IPO Debt Funds Real Assets SPVH Operates Here
Category III AIF
Hedge & Long-Short Equity

Funds that employ diverse or complex trading strategies and may employ leverage, including investments in listed or unlisted derivatives. Targets absolute returns across market cycles.

Eligibility & Regulatory Framework

Who Can Invest. What Governs It.

SEBI has defined clear eligibility criteria and a robust regulatory framework for AIF investments. Here is what you need to know before committing capital.

Eligible Investor Categories
High Net-Worth Individuals (HNIs) — resident Indians with minimum ₹1 Cr investable surplus and risk appetite for illiquid alternatives
Ultra HNIs (UHNIs) & Family Offices — single and multi-family offices with institutional-grade governance seeking private market exposure
Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) — can invest via NRE / NRO accounts subject to FEMA regulations and applicable tax treaties
Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) — registered FPIs and foreign institutions with appropriate SEBI clearances
Corporates & Trusts — duly incorporated companies, LLPs, and registered trusts meeting the accredited investor criteria
Institutional Investors — insurance companies, provident funds, endowments, and regulated entities seeking uncorrelated return streams
