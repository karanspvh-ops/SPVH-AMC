Investment Philosophy

We Invest Like It's Our Own Capital.

At SPVH AMC, our investment philosophy is not a marketing document — it is a living framework that governs every rupee we manage. It has been shaped by decades of market experience, multiple economic cycles, and an unwavering commitment to our investors' long-term interests.

We believe that sustainable wealth creation requires three things above all else — intellectual honesty, research depth, and the courage to be different when the data supports it. Our philosophy is not trend-chasing. It is not benchmark-hugging. It is a disciplined, repeatable process designed to compound capital across market cycles.

We define risk as the permanent loss of capital — not short-term mark-to-market volatility. This distinction shapes everything from how we size positions to how we communicate with investors during turbulent markets.

“

The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient.

— Warren Buffett
Research — Bottom-up, proprietary, scuttlebutt-driven
Conviction — Concentrated bets in our highest-quality ideas
Patience — Hold through cycles, exit on thesis change
Capital Preservation — Never lose permanently. Volatility is not risk.
Our Framework

Four Pillars of Our Investment Framework.

01
Fundamental Research

We build our own models. We visit companies. We talk to managements, competitors, and suppliers. Our research is 100% proprietary, deeply sourced, and independent of Wall Street consensus.

  • Bottom-up stock analysis from first principles
  • Scuttlebutt research methodology
  • Management meeting & channel checks
02
Portfolio Construction

We build concentrated, high-conviction portfolios — typically 15–25 positions in equity strategies. Each position is sized by conviction level and risk-adjusted return potential, not index weight.

  • Conviction-weighted position sizing (2–12%)
  • Sector & macro overlay for diversification
  • Liquidity screening at every position
03
Risk Management

Capital preservation is paramount. We define risk as permanent loss of capital — not short-term volatility. Every portfolio has hard risk limits, concentration caps, and drawdown triggers built in.

  • Strategy-level drawdown limits
  • Continuous portfolio stress testing
  • Daily risk monitoring by dedicated risk officer
04
Exit Discipline

Knowing when to sell is as important as knowing what to buy. We exit when the investment thesis breaks, valuation becomes unreasonable, or a materially better opportunity presents itself.

  • Pre-defined thesis-driven exit triggers
  • Valuation discipline at both entry and exit
  • Disciplined gain recycling framework

Responsible Investing

We Invest Responsibly.

We integrate Environmental, Social, and Governance factors into our investment analysis — not as a checkbox exercise, but as a genuine risk and opportunity lens. Companies with strong ESG practices tend to be better managed, more resilient, and more sustainable long-term compounders.

Our ESG integration is embedded in the due diligence process for every investment across every strategy — from listed equity PMS to pre-IPO AIF to offshore mandates. Governance quality, in particular, is a non-negotiable screen for every capital deployment decision.

Learn More About Our ESG Approach
Environmental
Resource efficiency, carbon footprint, ecological impact, and climate transition risk assessed at the company and portfolio level.
Social
Labour practices, community relations, supply chain ethics, and employee welfare as indicators of long-term cultural health.
Governance
Board independence, capital allocation discipline, promoter integrity, related-party transaction scrutiny, and minority shareholder rights.
