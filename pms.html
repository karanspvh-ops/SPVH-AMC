What is PMS?

Direct Ownership.
Personalised Strategy.

Portfolio Management Services (PMS) is a professional investment service where a SEBI-registered portfolio manager constructs and actively manages an equity portfolio on your behalf — directly in your demat account. Unlike pooled vehicles, you own every stock individually, with full visibility and the ability to customise around legacy holdings and tax positions.

At SPVH AMC, our PMS is built on deep primary research, concentrated high-conviction positions of 15–25 stocks, and a strict absolute-return discipline. We invest in businesses, not tickers.

Our Investment Approach

How We Build Your Portfolio.

Every holding in your portfolio is the output of a disciplined, repeatable research process built on three foundational pillars.

Proprietary Primary Research
Every idea originates from our own research — management meetings, channel checks, supplier interviews, and regulatory filings. We never buy based on broker calls or third-party tips.
Valuation Discipline
Quality at the right price. We run proprietary DCF models, earnings normalisation, and cross-cycle valuation checks to ensure we are never overpaying — even for a great business.
Rigorous Risk Management
Every position has a defined entry thesis, price target, stop-loss trigger, and exit condition. Portfolio-level risk — concentration, sector, liquidity — is monitored daily by a dedicated risk officer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know.

What is the minimum investment for SPVH PMS?
As mandated by SEBI's PMS Regulations 2020, the minimum investment is ₹50 Lakhs. This can be brought in as cash or as a portfolio of approved listed securities transferred in-kind.
How is PMS different from a Mutual Fund?
In a mutual fund, you hold units in a pooled vehicle with no direct ownership of stocks. In PMS, you hold individual stocks directly in your own demat account — giving you complete transparency, the ability to customise, and investor-level tax efficiency.
Can I retain or exclude specific stocks in my PMS portfolio?
Yes. Within the constraints of the active strategy, we can accommodate requests to retain legacy holdings (e.g., founder stock), exclude specific sectors, or defer selling appreciated positions for tax reasons. All customisations are documented in your investment agreement.
Who holds custody of my securities?
Your securities are held in a demat account in your own name, managed by an independent SEBI-registered custodian. SPVH AMC has a limited Power of Attorney (POA) only for transacting as per the agreed investment mandate — your assets are never commingled with our books.
How do I track my PMS portfolio performance?
You receive detailed monthly performance reports including attribution analysis, sector breakdown, and benchmark comparison. Additionally, you have 24/7 real-time access to your portfolio via our investor portal with live NAV tracking and transaction history.
What are the tax implications for PMS investments?
Since you own stocks directly, each transaction is a taxable event in your hands — subject to STCG (15%) or LTCG (10% above ₹1 Lakh) depending on holding period. This is an advantage: you can do tax-loss harvesting, time your capital gains, and manage across financial years in a way that pooled funds cannot offer. Our team provides guidance on tax-efficient portfolio management.
Can NRIs invest in SPVH PMS?
Yes. Non-Resident Indians can invest through NRE (fully repatriable), NRO (partially repatriable), or PINS (Portfolio Investment Scheme) accounts as permitted under RBI/FEMA guidelines. All DTAA benefits applicable to your country of residence are managed accordingly.
What is the exit process and are there lock-ins?
PMS is not a locked-in product. You may exit at any time with written notice. An exit load of 1% applies if you withdraw within 12 months of investment. Thereafter, withdrawal is free of exit load. Proceeds are typically settled within 3–5 working days after liquidation.
